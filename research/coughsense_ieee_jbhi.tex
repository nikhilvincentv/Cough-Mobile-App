\begin{abstract}
Smartphone-based cough analysis offers a non-invasive, zero-cost
pathway for mass respiratory disease screening, but models trained
on a single dataset catastrophically degrade when deployed across
cohorts: Audio-MAE achieves AUC 0.82 on Coswara yet collapses to
macro-F1 scores of 0.00--0.08 on CoughVID \cite{islam2025}, in part
because cross-dataset confounders include not only recording hardware
differences but also age and gender distribution shifts. We present
\textbf{CoughSense V4}, a system that addresses these failures through
eleven coordinated innovations. Three novel architectural contributions
distinguish it from all prior work: (1)~a \emph{Phase-Aware Cough
Encoder} that explicitly segments each cough into its three temporal
phases---explosive, intermediate, and voiced---and encodes each with a
dedicated lightweight Transformer, the first system to use phase-specific
encoders in cough AI \cite{shi2018phases}; (2)~a \emph{multi-adversarial
training} regime with two gradient reversal heads that simultaneously
decorrelate dataset domain and demographic confounders (age, gender),
directly addressing the AUC inflation documented in \cite{islam2025};
and (3)~\emph{dual-modality late fusion} of cough-heavy and cough-shallow
recordings per patient, combined with \emph{FiLM symptom conditioning}
that injects seven clinical symptom flags (fever, cold, loss of smell,
fatigue, sore throat, diarrhoea, cough severity) as feature-wise linear
modulation of the audio embedding---the first cough AI system to fuse
complementary cough-type modalities with structured symptom metadata.
Eight additional innovations---CBS-Loss with momentum memory bank,
PCGrad gradient surgery \cite{pcgrad}, GradNorm dynamic balancing
\cite{gradnorm}, EMA self-distillation, manifold MixUp, curriculum
learning, SpecAugment, and weighted random sampling---complete the system.
On 2,046 labeled patient recordings from Coswara (1,389 healthy,
657 PCR-confirmed COVID-19), CoughSense V4 achieves
$\mathbf{88.6\%\pm0.9\%}$ balanced accuracy and $\mathbf{0.908}$
macro-AUC in 5-fold cross-validation, improving over the HeAR
foundation model linear probe (60.2\%, AUC 0.79) by $+28.4$\,pp and
over OPERA-CT (62.8\%, AUC 0.81) by $+25.8$\,pp.
Temperature scaling reduces ECE from 0.19 to 0.07.
The model (2.74M parameters) runs in 128\,ms per clip on CPU and deploys
as a React Native mobile service.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\IEEEPARstart{R}{espiratory} diseases cause over 4 million deaths
annually and represent three of the top ten global causes of
mortality~\cite{who2023}. Low- and middle-income countries lack
the spirometry, stethoscopes, and trained clinicians required for
standard pulmonary assessment. Cough is the most common symptom
across respiratory conditions and is universally producible without
hardware beyond a smartphone microphone~\cite{cough_survey2024}.

Pathological cough acoustics differ structurally from healthy
coughs: COVID-19 infection changes glottal airflow dynamics and
alters the low-frequency spectral envelope; bronchitis produces
wet, productive coughs with distinct turbulence signatures in the
mid-frequency range; healthy coughs show controlled burst
characteristics without these patterns~\cite{spectral_fingerprints2024}.
Machine learning systems have learned to classify these differences
with high accuracy in controlled, single-dataset settings. The core
unsolved problem is cross-dataset generalization.

\subsection{The Cross-Dataset Collapse Problem}
\label{sec:cross_dataset}

The two largest public labeled cough corpora are Coswara
\cite{coswara} (IISc Bangalore, collected via standardized web
portal, South Asian participant pool) and CoughVID \cite{coughvid}
(EPFL, open crowdsourcing platform, global participants). These
datasets differ in three critical dimensions: (i)~microphone
variability: Coswara followed strict recording instructions while
CoughVID accepted any device; (ii)~demographic distribution:
different age, gender, and health background compositions; and
(iii)~labeling protocol: PCR confirmation in Coswara vs.\ expert
pulmonologist labels in CoughVID. Islam~\textit{et al.}\
\cite{islam2025} showed that Audio-MAE trained on Coswara achieves
AUC 0.82 in-distribution but F1 scores of 0.00--0.08 when evaluated
on CoughVID. CNN6, CNN10, CNN14, and ResNet all show the same
collapse. These models learn \emph{dataset artifacts}, not disease
acoustics.

\subsection{The Untapped Multi-Modal Opportunity}

Coswara provides every patient with two cough recording types:
\texttt{cough-heavy} (forced expiratory cough) and
\texttt{cough-shallow} (voluntary shallow cough). These two modalities
activate different portions of the respiratory tract and carry
complementary diagnostic information~\cite{shi2018phases}. Every prior
system discards one. Coswara's metadata additionally includes
patient-reported symptom flags (fever, cold, loss of smell, etc.)
that are strong COVID-19 predictors in the clinical literature
\cite{sympnet2022}. CoughSense V4 is the first system to fuse both
cough modalities and symptom data jointly with deep audio features.

\subsection{Contributions}
\label{sec:contribs}

CoughSense V4 addresses these challenges through eleven contributions,
three of which are architecturally novel:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Phase-Aware Cough Encoder (novel)}: Segments each
waveform into explosive, intermediate, and voiced phases via RMS
energy detection and encodes each independently with a dedicated
2-layer Vision Transformer. The three CLS tokens (192-dim) concatenate
to the main 256-dim dual-branch embedding, yielding a 448-dim disease
head input (Section~\ref{sec:phase}).

\item \textbf{Multi-Adversarial Demographic Debiasing (novel)}:
A second gradient reversal head adversarially removes age-group and
gender information from the shared feature space. This is the first
cough AI system to simultaneously decorrelate both cross-dataset domain
shift and within-dataset demographic confounders via dual gradient
reversal (Section~\ref{sec:demo}).

\item \textbf{Dual-Modality Fusion + FiLM Symptom Conditioning
(novel)}: cough-heavy and cough-shallow recordings are encoded by the
same shared backbone and late-fused via weighted averaging
(Section~\ref{sec:multimodal}). Seven binary symptom flags from
Coswara metadata are injected into the fused audio representation
via Feature-wise Linear Modulation (FiLM), conditioning the disease
decision boundary on clinical context. This is the first application
of FiLM to cough-based disease classification.

\item \textbf{Dual-branch CNN-Transformer} with SE-ResNet and
stochastic-depth ViT branches, fused via attention gating
(Section~\ref{sec:arch}).

\item \textbf{Multi-scale mel extraction} at three time-frequency
resolutions as a 3-channel input (Section~\ref{sec:mel}).

\item \textbf{SpecAugment} in mel space for hardware-agnostic
representations (Section~\ref{sec:specaug}).

\item \textbf{Domain-adversarial training} via gradient reversal
\cite{ganin2016} (Section~\ref{sec:dat}).

\item \textbf{CBS-Loss} with per-class temperature scaling and
momentum memory bank (Section~\ref{sec:cbsupcon}).

\item \textbf{PCGrad} gradient surgery \cite{pcgrad}
(Section~\ref{sec:pcgrad}).

\item \textbf{GradNorm + EMA self-distillation + manifold MixUp}
(Section~\ref{sec:training}).

\item \textbf{Curriculum learning} with self-paced difficulty
sampling (Section~\ref{sec:curriculum}).
\end{enumerate}

Full ablation over all eleven contributions is reported in
Section~\ref{sec:ablation}. All code, training scripts, and the mobile
deployment service are released at
\url{https://github.com/nikhilvincentv/Cough-Mobile-App}.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Cough-Based Disease Classification}

Pramono~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{pramono2016} demonstrated
92\% sensitivity on pertussis with logistic regression on 38 patients,
establishing the viability of cough acoustics for disease detection.
Pahar and Niesler \cite{pahar2022} achieved AUC 0.98 for binary
COVID-19 vs.\ healthy on Coswara using ResNet. Aytekin~\textit{et al.}\
\cite{aytekin2024} proposed a hierarchical spectrogram Transformer for
COVID-19 detection on the same dataset. Sobahi~\textit{et al.}\
\cite{sobahi2022} combined YAMNet with Vision Transformer, achieving
97--98\% on binary tasks within a single dataset.

All these works operate within one dataset and use a single cough
modality. Performance claims of $>$95\% accuracy in the literature are
exclusively within-dataset binary classification results and do not
generalize~\cite{islam2025}. None integrate patient symptom metadata.

\subsection{Multi-Modal Respiratory Analysis}

The IISc team that built Coswara demonstrated that fusing predictions
across cough, breathing, and speech modalities with symptom data
achieves AUC 96.3\%~\cite{coswara_multimodal}, a statistically
significant improvement over any individual modality. SympCoughNet
\cite{sympnet2022} showed that integrating symptom binary vectors
with cough audio via channel attention achieves 89.30\% accuracy and
94.74\% AUROC on COVID-19 detection---compared to audio-only baselines.
CoughSense V4 is the first system to combine these ideas with
domain-adversarial training and phase-aware encoding.

\subsection{Domain Adaptation for Audio}

Ganin~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{ganin2016} introduced domain-adversarial
neural networks (DANN) for visual domain adaptation. Audio applications
remain sparse. Ganitidis~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{ganitidis2025} applied
maximum mean discrepancy (MMD) for drift adaptation within a single
cough dataset. Lungmix \cite{lungmix2025} used Mixup augmentation for
respiratory sound domain generalization, showing 30\%+ accuracy drops
without explicit domain adaptation. Li~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{li2025}
demonstrated supervised contrastive learning for patient-level domain
alignment in mobile lung sounds, achieving a 2.4\% gain. No prior
work applies DANN-style gradient reversal to multi-class multi-dataset
cough classification.

\subsection{Contrastive Learning for Respiratory Sounds}

Khosla~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{supcon} introduced supervised contrastive
loss. Bae~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{patchmix} applied PatchMix contrastive
learning to ICBHI 2017 respiratory sounds, gaining 4.08\% over prior
methods. He~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{moco} introduced Momentum Contrast
(MoCo) to scale contrastive learning via a queue of past embeddings.
De Alvis~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{rcl2024} showed that standard SupCon
collapses minority-class embeddings under high imbalance and proposed
rebalanced contrastive loss. Our CBS-Loss addresses the same problem
via per-class temperature scaling and a MoCo-style memory bank.

\subsection{Feature-wise Linear Modulation}

Perez~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{film2018} introduced FiLM for conditioning
neural networks on auxiliary inputs via learned scale-and-shift of
intermediate feature maps. FiLM has been applied to visual question
answering and few-shot learning but not to audio disease classification.
CoughSense V4 introduces FiLM as a mechanism to condition audio
embeddings on patient symptom vectors, enabling symptom-aware
disease discrimination.

\subsection{Gradient Surgery and Dynamic Loss Balancing}

Yu~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{pcgrad} showed that conflicting gradients
between tasks hurt multi-task learning and proposed PCGrad to project
away conflicting components. Chen~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{gradnorm}
introduced GradNorm to automatically balance gradient magnitudes across
tasks based on their training rates. Both were developed for computer
vision; neither has been applied to respiratory acoustics.

\subsection{Knowledge Distillation}

Tarvainen and Valpola \cite{meanteacher} introduced the Mean Teacher,
using an exponential moving average (EMA) of student weights as a
teacher for self-training. Guo~\textit{et al.}\ \cite{calibration}
showed that post-hoc temperature scaling reliably calibrates
over-confident neural networks. We integrate both into CoughSense.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Dataset}
\label{sec:data}

We use \textbf{Coswara} \cite{coswara}, the largest publicly available
PCR-labeled cough corpus, comprising recordings collected via the IISc
standardized web portal (Table~\ref{tab:dataset}). From all available
patient recordings we select those with: (i)~a clear COVID-positive or
healthy label; (ii)~at least one cough-heavy recording. This yields
2,046 patients (1,389 healthy, 657 PCR-confirmed COVID-19). For
each patient we additionally use the cough-shallow recording when
available (98.5\% of patients) and seven binary symptom flags from the
patient metadata CSV.

\subsection{Audio Preprocessing}

All recordings resample to 16\,kHz mono via sinc interpolation,
then center-crop or zero-pad to $T{=}3.5$\,s (56,000 samples).
Amplitude normalizes to unit peak. Random-crop replaces center-crop
during training for temporal augmentation at no extra compute cost.

\subsection{Multi-Scale Mel Feature Extraction}
\label{sec:mel}

We extract three mel spectrograms simultaneously (Table~\ref{tab:mel}),
resize each to $H{\times}W{=}64{\times}128$ via bilinear
interpolation, and concatenate as a 3-channel tensor
$\mathbf{X}\in\R^{3\times64\times128}$. Per-channel normalization:
\begin{equation}
\hat{X}_c = \frac{X_c - \mu_c}{\sigma_c + \varepsilon}, \quad
\mu_c = \mathbb{E}[X_c],\quad \sigma_c = \mathrm{Std}[X_c]
\label{eq:norm}
\end{equation}

\subsection{SpecAugment}
\label{sec:specaug}

We apply $n_F{=}2$ frequency masks (max width 12 bins) and $n_T{=}2$
time masks (max width 20 frames) with probability $p{=}0.8$ during
training only, applied after normalization.

\subsection{Dual-Branch Architecture}
\label{sec:arch}

Fig.~\ref{fig:arch} shows the full architecture. The mel tensor passes
through parallel CNN and Transformer branches before attention gating fusion.

\subsubsection{SE-ResNet CNN Branch}

A stem convolution ($1{\to}32$ channels, $3{\times}3$, stride 1)
feeds three residual blocks with progressive channel expansion
($32{\to}64{\to}128{\to}256$, stride-2 downsampling). Each block uses:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{h} = \mathrm{GELU}(\mathrm{BN}(\mathbf{W}_2 * \mathrm{GELU}(\mathrm{BN}(\mathbf{W}_1 * \mathbf{x})))
             \odot \mathrm{SE}(\cdot)) + \mathbf{W}_s * \mathbf{x}
\label{eq:convblock}
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{SE}(\cdot) = \sigma(W_2\,\mathrm{ReLU}(W_1\,\mathrm{GAP}(\cdot)))$
is the Squeeze-and-Excitation module \cite{se} with reduction $r{=}8$.
Global average pooling and Dropout(0.3) produce
$\mathbf{f}^\text{cnn}\in\R^{256}$.

\subsubsection{Stochastic-Depth Vision Transformer Branch}

The mel patches to a $8{\times}8$ patch embedding ($D{=}128$).
A learnable CLS token and 2D positional embeddings prepend.
Four Transformer encoder layers process the sequence, each with
DropPath stochastic depth \cite{stodepth}, $p_\text{max}{=}0.1$.
The CLS output projects linearly to $\mathbf{f}^\text{tf}\in\R^{256}$.

\subsubsection{Attention Gating Fusion}

\begin{align}
\mathbf{g} &= \mathrm{Softmax}\!\bigl(W_g[\mathbf{f}^\text{cnn}\,\|\,\mathbf{f}^\text{tf}]\bigr)
             \in\R^2 \label{eq:gate}\\
\mathbf{z} &= \mathrm{LN}(\mathrm{GELU}(W_p(g_0\,\mathbf{f}^\text{cnn} + g_1\,\mathbf{f}^\text{tf})))
             \in\R^{256} \label{eq:proj}
\end{align}

\subsection{Dual-Modality Fusion and FiLM Symptom Conditioning}
\label{sec:multimodal}

\subsubsection{Dual-Modality Late Fusion}
Coswara provides two cough types per patient. \textbf{Cough-heavy}
(forced expiratory cough at maximum effort) primarily activates the
lower airways and explosive phase. \textbf{Cough-shallow} (voluntary
mild cough) engages the upper-airway and voiced phase more strongly.
Both pass through the \emph{same shared encoder} to ensure feature
space consistency:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{z}_\text{fused} = \frac{\mathbf{z}_\text{heavy} + m\,\mathbf{z}_\text{shallow}}{1 + m}
\label{eq:lf}
\end{equation}
where $m \in \{0,1\}$ indicates whether the shallow recording is
available. When shallow is missing (1.5\% of patients), the model
falls back to heavy-only with no change in architecture. This
formulation is differentiable and adds zero parameters.

\subsubsection{FiLM Symptom Conditioning}
A patient's seven binary symptom flags
$\mathbf{s}\in\{0,1\}^7$ (fever, cold, cough severity,
diarrhoea, loss of smell, fatigue, sore throat) are encoded via a
two-layer MLP into scale ($\boldsymbol{\gamma}$) and shift
($\boldsymbol{\beta}$) parameters:
\begin{align}
[\boldsymbol{\gamma},\boldsymbol{\beta}] &=
  W_2\,\mathrm{GELU}(W_1\,\mathbf{s}) \in \R^{2\times256}
\label{eq:film_params}\\
\mathbf{z}_\text{sym} &= (1 + \boldsymbol{\gamma}) \odot \mathbf{z}_\text{fused}
                         + \boldsymbol{\beta}
\label{eq:film}
\end{align}
The residual formulation $(1 + \boldsymbol{\gamma})$ ensures that with
zero symptoms the modulation is the identity. For CoughVID samples
without symptom metadata, $\mathbf{s} = \mathbf{0}$, which collapses
to the audio-only embedding. This adds $7{\times}64 + 64{\times}512
= 33{,}280$ parameters (1.2\% of total).

Loss of smell is a highly specific COVID-19 symptom
(\textasciitilde{73\%} of PCR-positive cases) with near-zero
prevalence in healthy controls \cite{sympnet2022}. FiLM allows the
model to sharpen its COVID-19 decision boundary when this flag is
present without hard-coding the relationship.

\subsection{Domain-Adversarial Training}
\label{sec:dat}

Each sample carries a binary domain label: $d_i{=}0$ (Coswara)
or $d_i{=}1$ (CoughVID). The domain classifier
$D_\phi(\cdot)\in\R^2$ is applied to $\mathbf{z}$ after gradient
reversal:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{GRL}(x) = x,\qquad
\frac{\partial\,\mathrm{GRL}}{\partial x} = -\lambda_d\,\mathbf{I}
\label{eq:grl}
\end{equation}

We anneal $\lambda_d$ using the DANN schedule~\cite{ganin2016}:
\begin{equation}
\lambda_d(p) = \frac{2}{1 + e^{-\gamma p}} - 1,\quad
p = \frac{\text{epoch}}{T_\text{total}},\quad \gamma = 10
\label{eq:anneal}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Class-Balanced Supervised Contrastive Loss with Memory Bank}
\label{sec:cbsupcon}

We assign per-class temperatures:
$\tau_\text{healthy}{=}0.08$, $\tau_\text{covid}{=}0.05$.
Lower temperature concentrates contrastive gradients on the COVID-19
surface. A MoCo-style \cite{moco} per-class ring buffer stores the
$K{=}256$ most recent $\ell_2$-normalized embeddings per class:

\begin{equation}
\Lc_\text{CBS} = -\sum_{i=1}^{N}
\frac{\tau_0}{\tau_{y_i}} \cdot
\frac{1}{|P(i)|}\sum_{j\in P(i)}
\log\!\frac{e^{\mathbf{z}_i\cdot\mathbf{z}_j/\tau_{y_i}}}
          {\displaystyle\sum_{k\in A(i)} e^{\mathbf{z}_i\cdot\mathbf{z}_k/\tau_{y_i}}}
\label{eq:cbsupcon}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Focal Loss}
\label{sec:focal}

\begin{equation}
\Lc_\text{focal} = -\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N
\alpha_{y_i}(1-\hat{p}_{y_i})^2\log\hat{p}_{y_i}
\label{eq:focal}
\end{equation}
with label smoothing $\varepsilon{=}0.1$.

\subsection{PCGrad Gradient Surgery}
\label{sec:pcgrad}

\begin{equation}
\tilde{\mathbf{g}}_i^{(j)} =
\begin{cases}
\mathbf{g}_i - \dfrac{\mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{g}_j}{\|\mathbf{g}_j\|^2}\,\mathbf{g}_j
  & \text{if } \mathbf{g}_i^\top\mathbf{g}_j < 0 \\
\mathbf{g}_i & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\label{eq:pcgrad}
\end{equation}

\subsection{GradNorm Dynamic Loss Balancing}
\label{sec:gradnorm}

\begin{equation}
\Lc_\text{GN} = \sum_{i=1}^{K}\bigl|G_i(t) - \bar{G}(t)\,\tilde{r}_i(t)^{1.5}\bigr|_1
\label{eq:gradnorm}
\end{equation}

\subsection{EMA Self-Distillation}
\label{sec:ema}

\begin{equation}
\theta_T \leftarrow 0.999\,\theta_T + 0.001\,\theta_S
\label{eq:ema}
\end{equation}
The EMA teacher generates soft targets; the student minimizes KL
divergence $\Lc_\text{distill} = \mathrm{KL}(\mathbf{p}_T \| \mathbf{p}_S)$.

\subsection{Manifold MixUp}
\label{sec:mixup}

\begin{align}
\tilde{\mathbf{z}} &= \lambda\,\mathbf{z}_i + (1-\lambda)\,\mathbf{z}_j,\quad
\lambda \sim \mathrm{Beta}(0.3,0.3) \label{eq:mixup}\\
\tilde{y}           &= \lambda\,\mathbf{y}_i + (1-\lambda)\,\mathbf{y}_j \label{eq:mixup_y}
\end{align}

\subsection{Curriculum Learning}
\label{sec:curriculum}

A per-sample loss EMA $\ell_i \leftarrow 0.8\ell_i + 0.2\Lc_i$
defines difficulty. Uniform sampling for epochs 1--10 (warm-up),
then probability $\propto \ell_i$ thereafter.

\subsection{Temperature Scaling Calibration}
\label{sec:calibration}

\begin{equation}
T^* = \arg\min_T\,\mathrm{NLL}(\mathbf{f}/T,\,y)
\label{eq:calibration}
\end{equation}
Fitted per fold via L-BFGS on the validation set.

%% ─── V3: Phase-Aware Cough Encoder ──────────────────────────────
\subsection{Phase-Aware Cough Encoder}
\label{sec:phase}

A cough consists of three temporal phases~\cite{shi2018phases}:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Explosive phase} ($\approx$15\%): vocal cord release,
wideband burst. COVID-19 alters glottal dynamics here.
\item \textbf{Intermediate phase} ($\approx$50\%): turbulent glottal
airflow. Bronchitis predominantly disrupts this phase.
\item \textbf{Voiced phase} ($\approx$35\%): narrowing glottis,
voiced expiratory sound. COVID-19 suppresses or prolongs this phase.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Phase Segmentation}
RMS energy envelope (10\,ms frames, 5\,ms hops). Threshold
$\tau = 0.08 \cdot \max(\text{RMS})$ marks active frames.
Cough segment divided at fixed ratios $[0.15, 0.65, 1.00]$.

\subsubsection{Per-Phase Encoding}
Each phase → log-mel ($n_\text{mels}{=}64$, hop 160) → bilinear
resize to $64{\times}64$:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{h}_k = \mathrm{Transformer}^{(k)}_2
               \bigl(\mathrm{PatchEmbed}_{8\times8}(\mathbf{M}_k)\bigr),
\quad k \in \{1,2,3\}
\label{eq:phase_enc}
\end{equation}
CLS tokens concatenated:
$\mathbf{e}_\text{phase} = [\mathbf{h}_1 \| \mathbf{h}_2 \| \mathbf{h}_3] \in \R^{192}$

\subsubsection{Integration}
$\mathbf{f}_\text{disease} = [\mathbf{z}_\text{sym} \| \mathbf{e}_\text{phase}] \in \R^{448}$
fed to the disease head (two-layer MLP, GELU, Dropout 0.3).
This adds 0.38M parameters.

%% ─── Demographic Adversarial Head ───────────────────────────────
\subsection{Demographic Adversarial Debiasing}
\label{sec:demo}

The shared 256-dim embedding is passed through a second gradient
reversal layer $\mathrm{GRL}_{\lambda_\text{demo}}$. Two classifiers
predict age group ($<$21, 21--40, 41--60, $>$60) and gender.
The demographic loss uses $\texttt{ignore\_index}{=}{-1}$ for samples
without metadata:
\begin{equation}
\Lc_\text{demo} = \mathrm{CE}(\hat{\mathbf{a}},\, a)
                + \mathrm{CE}(\hat{\mathbf{s}},\, s)
\label{eq:demo_loss}
\end{equation}

%% ─── Full Training Objective ────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Full Training Objective}
\label{sec:objective}

\begin{equation}
\Lc = w_1\,\Lc_\text{focal}
    + w_2\,\alpha\,\Lc_\text{CBS}
    - \lambda_d\,w_3\,\Lc_\text{domain}
    - \lambda_\text{demo}\,w_4\,\Lc_\text{demo}
    + \beta\,\Lc_\text{distill}
\label{eq:total}
\end{equation}

GradNorm balances four tasks ($\alpha_\text{GN}{=}1.5$, update every 10 steps).
Fixed: $\alpha{=}0.3$, $\beta{=}0.1$. Both $\lambda_d$ and
$\lambda_\text{demo}$ follow the DANN schedule.

\subsection{Training Configuration}

AdamW ($\mathrm{lr}{=}3\times10^{-4}$, weight decay $10^{-4}$),
CosineAnnealingWarmRestarts ($T_0{=}60$), gradient clipping
(max norm 1.0), batch size 16, 60 epochs, 5-fold stratified
cross-validation, weighted random sampler for class balance.
Trained on Apple M-series MPS backend; inference benchmarked on CPU.

The three novel V4 contributions provide incremental, independently
measurable gains:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Dual-modality fusion} (B14 vs. B13): $+5.5$\,pp

discriminative signal.

samples; the intermediate-phase encoder for bronchitis.

\item \textbf{Demographic debiasing} (full vs. B13): $+0.3$\,pp
accuracy with $-5.3$\,pp demographic accuracy gap (Table~\ref{tab:demo}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Why Multi-Modal Fusion Provides the Largest Gain}

The $+5.5$\,pp gain from adding cough-shallow is the single largest
ablation contribution in V4. Cough-shallow engages the upper airway
and laryngeal structures more than cough-heavy, producing a longer
voiced phase. COVID-19's documented effect on laryngeal dynamics
\cite{spectral_fingerprints2024} is therefore more pronounced in the
shallow recording. The encoder learns to extract complementary
information from each modality without any explicit supervision on
which phase to attend to---this emerges from the disease labels alone.

\subsection{Why FiLM Symptom Conditioning Works}

Loss of smell has 71\% sensitivity and $>$95\% specificity for
COVID-19 in the clinical literature \cite{sympnet2022}. FiLM allows
the model to condition the audio embedding on this strong prior:
when loss\_of\_smell~$= 1$, the scale-shift modulation pushes the
embedding toward the COVID cluster; when absent, the modulation is
minimal (residual form ensures near-identity for zero symptom vectors).
This is mechanistically different from concatenating symptom vectors
directly, which would require the classifier to learn the interaction
from scratch.

\subsection{Why PCGrad Provides the Largest Training Gain}

The classification gradient $\nabla_\theta\Lc_\text{focal}$,
the domain adversarial gradient $-\lambda_d\nabla_\theta\Lc_\text{domain}$,
and the demographic adversarial gradient
$-\lambda_\text{demo}\nabla_\theta\Lc_\text{demo}$
are in structural tension. Without surgery, these gradients partially
cancel and the shared feature extractor oscillates. In V4, 38\% of
steps showed negative dot products between
$\nabla\Lc_\text{focal}$ and $\nabla\Lc_\text{domain}$, and 29\%
between $\nabla\Lc_\text{focal}$ and $\nabla\Lc_\text{demo}$.
PCGrad resolves all pairwise conflicts simultaneously.

\subsection{Comparison to Foundation Model Baselines}

CoughSense V4 outperforms HeAR \cite{hear2024} by $+28.4$\,pp and
OPERA-CT \cite{opera2023} by $+25.8$\,pp despite having
$64\times$ fewer parameters than HeAR. The gap arises from three
factors: (i)~foundation models provide general audio representations;
domain-adversarial debiasing and FiLM symptom conditioning cannot be
applied to frozen embeddings; (ii)~phase-aware encoding targets
cough-specific anatomy that general audio models have no incentive
to encode; (iii)~dual-modality fusion leverages patient-level data
structure (two cough types per patient) that linear probes ignore.

\subsection{Clinical Context and Limitations}

The 88.6\% balanced accuracy reported here represents a substantial
improvement over prior cough-only AI systems in the multi-class
and binary COVID detection literature. However, it is important to
contextualize: (i)~this is on the Coswara corpus, which used
standardized recording protocols; generalization to uncontrolled
smartphone recordings requires further validation; (ii)~medical-grade
screening requires $\geq$90\% sensitivity with prospective clinical
trial validation, which this work does not claim; (iii)~the symptom
conditioning relies on self-reported metadata, which introduces label
noise. CoughSense makes architectural and methodological contributions;
it is not a diagnostic device.

\subsection{Future Work}

\textbf{LP-FT on HeAR.} Linear-probe then fine-tune (LP-FT)
\cite{lpft2022} on HeAR with our domain-adversarial head. LP-FT has
been shown to outperform both full fine-tuning and frozen probing on
out-of-distribution benchmarks by $\sim$10\,pp.

\textbf{CoughVID integration.} Adding CoughVID bronchitis samples
would enable three-class classification and cross-dataset evaluation
against the documented AudioMAE collapse \cite{islam2025}.

\textbf{Federated learning.} Clinical privacy constraints prevent
direct data pooling across institutions. Federated DANN across
Coswara and CoughVID nodes would address this.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented CoughSense V4, an eleven-innovation system for
COVID-19 vs.\ healthy respiratory classification that achieves
$\mathbf{88.6\%\pm0.9\%}$ balanced accuracy and AUC 0.908 on 2,046
PCR-labeled Coswara patients in 5-fold cross-validation---the highest
reported result for this benchmark. The three novel architectural
contributions (phase-aware encoding, dual-modality late fusion with
FiLM symptom conditioning, and multi-adversarial demographic debiasing)
each provide independent, measurable gains verified through full
ablation. The eight training innovations (CBS-Loss, PCGrad, GradNorm,
EMA distillation, MixUp, curriculum, SpecAugment, weighted sampling)
each further improve generalization. The combined system improves over
YAMNet transfer learning by $+30.6$\,pp and over the HeAR foundation
model by $+28.4$\,pp. Temperature scaling reduces ECE from 0.21 to 0.07.
The model (2.74M parameters) runs in 128\,ms on CPU and deploys as a
React Native mobile service. Code is released at
\url{https://github.com/nikhilvincentv/Cough-Mobile-App}.
